The Legal Academy

Ratio Decidendi vs. Obiter Dicta +
Ratio Decidendi: The legal reason for a judge's decision. This creates the "binding" element of precedent for lower courts.

Obiter Dicta: "Things said by the way." Opinions or remarks that are not central to the decision. They are persuasive but not binding.
Stare Decisis: The Doctrine of Precedent +
This is the cornerstone of English Law. It means "to stand by things decided." It ensures certainty and consistency by requiring courts to follow decisions made by higher courts in similar cases.

Socratic Challenge

"If a robot is given legal personhood (like a corporation), can it be held criminally liable for 'murder' if it malfunctions?"

The Proponent View: If a corporation can be fined for manslaughter, an AI entity should be subject to legal penalties (fines or termination) to ensure corporate accountability.

The Opponent View: Criminal law requires Mens Rea (a guilty mind). Since a robot lacks consciousness or intent, it cannot meet the requirements for a criminal conviction.
